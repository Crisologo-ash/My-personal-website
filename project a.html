Ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all

SDG 4, FOCUSED ON QUALITY EDUCATION, is vital for achieving other Sustainable Development Goals, yet global progress remains insufficient. As of 2019, only 58% of primary students achieved minimum reading proficiency, and learning outcomes, especially in math and reading, have declined in many countries, even before COVID-19. While primary and lower secondary completion rates are rising, upper secondary improvements have slowed, and disparities persist based on socioeconomic status, location, and gender. Early childhood development is on track for two-thirds of children in low- and middle-income countries, and pre-primary participation is rebounding after pandemic-related declines. However, adult education participation is low, especially for those over 25. Many schools lack essential infrastructure, particularly for students with disabilities and gender-specific needs, though access to electricity and internet is improving. Accelerating action on SDG 4 is crucial to unlocking progress across the broader 2030 Agenda.
